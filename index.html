Event One — Opening Session
Saturday • 10:00–11:30 • Main Hall
⌄

This interactive session introduces secondary school students to the neuroscience of motivation, focus and stress. Through the “Mind Mastery” programme, students will learn how the brain’s reward system influences achievement, time management and academic performance.

Students can expect practical tools they can immediately apply to school life, along with activities that build self-awareness and resilience. The session aims to strengthen confidence, reduce academic stress and promote brain-based learning.

Motivation Stress Management Brain-Based Learning
Event Two — Workshop
Saturday • 12:00–13:30 • Workshop Room
⌄

This seminar and hands-on workshop explores how early experiences shape a child’s brain development. Parents will learn how safe play, positive interaction and daily habits influence neural connections, emotional regulation and long-term cognitive growth.

Using three-dimensional brain and neuron models, the session makes complex neuroscience simple and practical. Parents can expect clear guidance on how to support healthy brain development at home and avoid common mistakes that may unintentionally hinder growth.
